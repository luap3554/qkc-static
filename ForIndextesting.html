Our Design & Build Process

A seamless, curated experience from first conversation to long-term care.

01

Discovery & Vision

We begin with a private consultation to understand your goals, lifestyle, and priorities. Together, we outline scope, budget range, and timeline. When needed, we recommend or coordinate with designers and architects to bring your vision to life.

02

Feasibility & Planning

Our team reviews your property, zoning, and site conditions, then provides estimated costs, risks, and opportunities so you can make informed decisions. This is the first part of the “go, no-go” stage. If aligned, we will sign a Design Agreement.

03

Concept & Design

We develop tailored layouts and design concepts, refining them until the direction is exactly right. Deliverables are designs and a corresponding itemized estimate.

04

Permitting & Contract

We manage all approvals and finalize a transparent contract with clear pricing and schedules.

05

Selections & Final Design

We coordinate with trusted vendors and artisans to help you choose finishes and fixtures. All selections are documented to ensure the design is fully build-ready.

06

Construction & Oversight

Your dedicated project manager oversees every step, ensuring craftsmanship, quality checks, and regular updates. Requested changes are formalized through change orders for full transparency.

07

Completion & Handover

We walk through your project together, resolve any final details, and provide warranties, manuals, and documentation.

08

Aftercare & Support

We follow up after move-in, handle warranty items promptly, and are available for maintenance advice to help you preserve your investment.

General Homeowner Advice

Communication

Regular, transparent updates maintain alignment between builder and client. A scheduled weekly call is best practice.

Budget

A 10–20% contingency is wise for unforeseen conditions.

Timeline

On average, projects take 4–16 weeks. New home builds can take 8-12 months.

Builder Selection

Work only with licensed, insured contractors. Nothing is more expensive than a cheap contractor!
